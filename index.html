What is Dowry?

Dowry is the practice of giving money, goods, or property by the bride's family to the groom's family at the time of marriage. This tradition has been practiced in various forms across different cultures for centuries.

Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 - Dowry Laws

Section 84 - Dowry Death:

If a woman dies within 7 years of marriage due to dowry harassment, it's considered dowry death.

Punishment:

Minimum 7 years imprisonment, extending to life imprisonment + Fine

Section 85 - Cruelty by Husband or Relatives:

Physical or mental cruelty for dowry demands is a punishable offense.

Punishment:

Up to 3 years imprisonment + Fine

Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961

Section 3 - Penalty for Giving or Taking Dowry:

Both giving and receiving dowry is illegal and punishable.

Punishment:

Minimum 5 years imprisonment + Fine of ₹15,000 or dowry amount (whichever is higher)

Section 4 - Penalty for Demanding Dowry:

Even demanding dowry directly or indirectly is a criminal offense.

Punishment:

Minimum 6 months to 2 years imprisonment + Fine up to ₹10,000

Other Relevant Laws

Indian Evidence Act - Section 113B:

Presumption of dowry death if a woman dies within 7 years of marriage under abnormal circumstances.

Code of Criminal Procedure - Section 174:

Mandatory police investigation in cases of unnatural death of women within 7 years of marriage.

Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005:

Provides civil remedies for dowry-related harassment and domestic violence.
